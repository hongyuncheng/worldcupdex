## Qualification Outlook

The 2026 World Cup sends the top two teams in each group to the Round of 32, plus the eight best third-placed teams. That format gives Iran more than one route forward, but the opening match remains the pivot.

A realistic group path looks like this:

- Beat New Zealand, or at minimum avoid losing the opener.
- Keep the Belgium match controlled; a draw would be a high-value result.
- Treat Egypt as a likely ranking and third-place tiebreaker match.

## Key Iran Players and Style Notes

Iran's squad pool includes familiar names such as Mehdi Taremi, Alireza Jahanbakhsh and Alireza Beiranvand. The team profile is usually built around defensive organization, direct attacking moments and set-piece value.

Group G creates three different tests: Belgium will test defensive quality, New Zealand will test Iran's ability to take initiative, and Egypt will test physical duels and late-game execution.
